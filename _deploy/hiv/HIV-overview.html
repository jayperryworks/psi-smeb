Overview

We identified 27 studies that assessed the effectiveness of social marketing on behavioral factors, behaviors, and health outcomes related to HIV.

  • 20 studies found changes in behavioral factors such as increases in risk perception, knowledge, and self-efficacy about HIV/AIDS.

  • 18 studies found changes in behaviors such increases up to 100% in condom use, 49% reductions in needle sharing among people who inject drugs, and increases in HIV testing. There were no significant effects for partner reduction.

  • 8 studies found changes in health outcomes such as reductions of up to 53% in HIV prevalence and 77% in STI prevalence, and 79% increase in STI cure rate.

A total of 16 out of 27 HIV studies had Strength of the Evidence scores of 4 or higher and 15 studies had Social Marketing Benchmark Criteria scores of 5 or higher.
